OUR FOUNDER'S STORY

The Practice That Changed Everything

"The medication never helped — it just made me feel like a zombie. Numb. The best thing I ever did was getting clean off the meds and getting healthy. Yoga changed everything."

Yoga4Autism was founded in 2013 by David Ellams — entrepreneur, technologist, public speaker and person on the autism spectrum — long before it was widely talked about, long before there was support, and long before the world had language for what he was experiencing.

After years of trying conventional medication that left him feeling disconnected and unlike himself, David discovered yoga. Then mindfulness. Then meditation. Then nutrition. Then nature. One by one, each piece of the holistic puzzle clicked into place — and his symptoms began to genuinely improve, not just be masked.

"I now do yoga every single day — religiously — because nothing has helped me more." From that transformation came a burning desire to share it: first through yoga classes, then school visits, public speaking at shows and events, retreats, 1-on-1 sessions and group classes — and eventually, a global franchise model to bring Y4A to every community on Earth.

Y4A was the very first project David created in 2013 — before the OASIS, before the Justice League Academy, before anything else. It is the seed from which an entire vision of a better world grew. And after nearly a decade of building the technology to support that vision, Y4A is back — and this time, it has the full power of the OASIS Omniverse behind it.

"I have been trying to get back to Y4A ever since I pivoted to the tech in 2016. It took 9 years longer than planned — but here we are. And I truly believe that everything we built in between was worth it, because now Y4A can reach the whole world."

David Ellams
CEO / CTO / FOUNDER — YOGA4AUTISM LTD & NEXTGEN WORLD LTD · CREATOR OF THE OASIS
WHY IT WORKS

The Science & the Experience

Decades of research — and thousands of lived experiences — show that yoga and holistic practices make a measurable difference for autistic people and those with related conditions. Here is what we see, session by session.

😌
REDUCED ANXIETY

Breathwork and gentle movement activate the parasympathetic nervous system — switching off the fight-or-flight response that many autistic people live in constantly.

🎯
IMPROVED FOCUS

Mindfulness and meditation train attention in a gentle, pressure-free way — often with profound results for those who struggle with traditional concentration exercises.

🤸
BETTER BODY AWARENESS

Many autistic people have a disconnected relationship with their own bodies. Yoga rebuilds that connection gently — improving coordination, proprioception and self-regulation.

😴
DEEPER SLEEP

Sleep difficulties are extremely common on the spectrum. Evening yoga and breathwork practices help calm an overactive nervous system and significantly improve sleep quality.

🗣️
SOCIAL CONFIDENCE

Group classes in a safe, non-judgmental space gently build the social comfort and communication skills that other environments can make feel overwhelming.

⚡
SENSORY REGULATION

Specific yoga sequences and mindfulness techniques help manage sensory overload — one of the most debilitating daily challenges for many people on the spectrum.
